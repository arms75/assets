Expressive Writing · Bounded Intensive

Two Weeks, Four Sessions

A finite block of structured processing — not a permanent habit. It starts, it does its work, it ends. The prompts move forward on purpose so you don't circle the part that hurts most.

CadenceSun · Wed · Sun · Wed — evenly spaced so each session settles before the next.
Length~20 min, soft timer. Stop when it rings even mid-sentence.
By handIf you can. Slower is the point — it forces processing over performance.
No editingNobody reads this. Don't tidy it. Tidy prose is avoidance.
Reading nightsOn writing nights, let before-bed reading go light or skip it. Don't stack.
Keep the anchorsTraining, SQL, Blender stay put. The writing fills gaps; it doesn't replace beams.
SESSION 01

What it was

Sun · Week 1

The honest inventory. An accurate ledger is what protects you on the nostalgic nights.

Write the relationship as it actually was — not as grief is currently rewriting it. Two columns, both real: what was genuinely good and what you valued in her and in being with her; and the frustration, the ways it wasn't what you wanted, the cost you were paying day to day.

Don't resolve the tension between the columns. Let both stand.

End on The thing I keep softening or leaving out when I tell this story is ___.
SESSION 02

What it protected me from

Wed · Week 1

The shadow session. Not about her — about you. This is the uncomfortable one; that's the signal it's the right one.

What did this relationship let me avoid looking at in myself? You've already named the edge of it — "protected from being alone in the world." Go in. What's the fear underneath it? When did it start? What would I have had to face about myself if she hadn't been there?

No fixing, no resolving. Just look at it directly and write down what you see.

End on The part of me that accepted this situation was trying to protect me from ___.
SESSION 03

The two griefs

Sun · Week 2

Mixed grief feels bottomless. Named and separated, it becomes two finite things with different remedies.

Write each grief separately and explicitly. First: grief for Molly herself — her company, who she is, the specific person. Then: grief for the loss of the buffer — being alone in the world without protection.

Which one is heavier? On what kind of day is it which? Be specific about how each one actually feels in the body.

End on When I look honestly, more of what I'm missing right now is ___ — and that matters because the remedy is different.
SESSION 04

What I'm building toward

Wed · Week 2

Forward-pointing, and deliberately last. Not "I'm fine now" — you'd know that's false. Where the energy goes next.

Given everything in the last three sessions: what do I actually want from connection? What would reciprocal feel like — concretely, not as an abstraction? What's one small thing in the next month that moves toward it? (Name the low-stakes social step here.)

Then re-read what you wrote about the reframe at the start of all this, and write it again in today's words. Let it have shifted.

End on I don't have to convert this into a gift. I just have to stop it being only a wound. Right now, the part that's no longer only a wound is ___.
When the two weeks are done, they're done. Don't extend the intensive — re-running the same material daily entrenches the loop rather than clearing it. The ongoing work moves to the weekly practice card from here. If a bad week hits later, that's not regression; it's a smaller wave, and the weekly card is built to hold it.
