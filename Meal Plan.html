Batch meals · each makes 4

1 · Red Lentil & Spinach Dahl

per portion (sauce): ~320 kcal · 17 g protein · 11 g fibre · ~5 mg iron · serve with ~200 g cooked rice

Fridge life: 4 days · best reheated with a splash of water in a pan

Ingredients

240 g dried red lentils · 1 onion · 3 garlic cloves · thumb ginger · 1 tin chopped tomatoes · 1 tin light coconut milk · 200 g spinach · 2 tbsp garam masala · 1 tsp cumin · 1 tsp turmeric · 1 veg stock cube · 1 tbsp oil · juice of ½ lemon

Method

  1. Soften onion, garlic & ginger in oil, 5 min.
  2. Add spices, stir 1 min.
  3. Add lentils, tomatoes, coconut milk, stock + 400 ml water.
  4. Simmer 25–30 min until lentils collapse.
  5. Wilt spinach through; finish with lemon, season lightly.
  6. Cool, divide into 4 tubs.

2 · Black Bean & Sweet Potato Chilli

per portion (sauce): ~330 kcal · 15 g protein · 15 g fibre · ~4.5 mg iron · serve with ~200 g rice or in a wrap

Fridge life: 4–5 days · thickens as it sits, loosen with a little water when reheating

Ingredients

2 tins black beans · 1 tin kidney beans · 2 sweet potatoes, diced · 1 onion · 2 garlic cloves · 1 red pepper · 1 tin chopped tomatoes · 2 tbsp tomato purée · 1–2 tbsp smoked paprika · 1 tsp cumin · 1 tsp oregano · chilli to taste · 1 veg stock cube · 1 square dark chocolate (optional) · 1 tbsp oil

Method

  1. Soften onion, garlic & pepper in oil.
  2. Add spices, 1 min.
  3. Add sweet potato, beans, tomatoes, purée, stock + 200 ml water.
  4. Simmer 30–35 min until sweet potato is tender and sauce thick.
  5. Stir in dark chocolate if using.
  6. Cool, divide into 4 tubs.

3 · Tofu & Mushroom Yaki Udon the lighter one

per portion: ~560 kcal · 25 g protein · noodle-based, slurpable · ~10 min fresh

Fridge life: 2 days (noodles soften) · best cooked fresh or keep sauce & noodles separate

Ingredients

4 nests udon noodles · 2 blocks (~560 g) firm tofu, pressed & cubed · 200 g shiitake or chestnut mushrooms · 1 red pepper · 150 g beansprouts · 4 spring onions · 2 garlic cloves · 2 eggs, beaten · 1 tbsp neutral oil
Sauce: 4 tbsp reduced-salt soy · 2 tbsp mirin · 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil · 1 tsp sugar · pinch white pepper

Method (fresh cook, ~10 min)

  1. Pan-fry tofu in neutral oil until golden; set aside.
  2. Cook udon per pack, drain.
  3. Stir-fry garlic, mushrooms & pepper on high heat 3–4 min.
  4. Push aside, pour in beaten egg, scramble lightly.
  5. Add noodles, tofu, beansprouts & sauce; toss 2 min until glossy.
  6. Finish with spring onion.
Sodium note: use reduced-salt soy — yaki udon is the one dish that nudges sodium up, and this keeps the win you've made.

Brothy alternative — Tofu & Mushroom Ramen (~15 min, fresh)

Same tofu & mushrooms, but build a broth: 1 L veg stock + 2 tbsp white miso + 1 tbsp reduced-salt soy + 1 tsp grated ginger + 1 garlic clove. Simmer, add cooked noodles, top with tofu, mushrooms, pak choi, a soft-boiled egg & spring onion.

Best made fresh — keep noodles separate from the broth if prepping ahead.

Broth only: 3 days · noodles: store separately, use within 2 days

Daily staples

Homemade fruit & nut bars · makes ~10

per bar: ~200 kcal · 8 g protein · no-bake · ~35p each vs ~£1+ shop-bought

Fridge life: 1–2 weeks · or freeze individually and grab as needed

Ingredients

200 g pitted dates · 150 g crunchy peanut butter · 120 g rolled oats · 60 g peanuts or mixed nuts · 40 g pumpkin seeds · 40 g sunflower seeds · 2 tbsp honey or golden syrup · pinch salt · optional 50 g dark chocolate to drizzle

Method

  1. If firm, soak dates in hot water 10 min, drain; blitz to a paste.
  2. Warm peanut butter + honey until loose.
  3. Mix everything until it clumps.
  4. Press very firmly into a lined 20×20 cm tin.
  5. Chill 2 hr, drizzle dark chocolate if using, cut into 10.
