Effort & RIR

  • Working sets before the last run at about 1 rep in reserve.
  • Last set to failure — on isolation and stable movements only.
  • Heavy barbell lifts (sumo, RDL, bent-over row) stay at 1–2 RIR, never to failure — most fatigue cost, least to gain from grinding.
  • Cycle 5 is the failure peak; back off into your deload after it.

Equipment gaps & cheap fixes

Vertical pulling — the one real hole

Without a bar there's no true pull-up or pulldown; the DB pullover only partly covers the lats. A doorway or wall-mounted pull-up bar is the single best addition — it restores chin-ups and brings back proper vertical pulling on both upper days.

A resistance band

Restores loaded hip abduction, and doubles as a band face pull and as assistance for chin-ups once you have a bar. Cheap, packs flat, fills several gaps at once.

Heavy bilateral squatting

No rack caps how much you can load on a back or front squat. Bulgarian split squats and goblet squats carry the quad load one leg at a time in the meantime — and the split squat in particular can be pushed surprisingly hard with just the DBs.
