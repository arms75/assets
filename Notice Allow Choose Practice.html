Ongoing Practice · Built to outlive the heartbreak

Notice · Allow · Choose

A standing ten-minute weekly review. Three movements drawn from three frameworks that aren't redundant — they're three stages of one move. Each ends by handing the feeling somewhere to go, which is precisely what rumination never does.

WHEN  Saturday, around the gym session
LENGTH  ~10 min
FORMAT  A few lines per movement — brevity is the control
NOTICE→ALLOW→CHOOSE
The opposite of rumination, which goes notice → re-feel → notice the same thing again.
Notice IFS · Parts
Name the part. "A part of me feels worthless" — not "I am worthless." The part is not the whole of you.
Ask What parts of me showed up this week, and what was each one trying to protect? Name them. Thank them. Don't argue with them.
Allow DBT · Radical acceptance
Let the feeling be there without fighting it. Resisting what's true adds a second layer of suffering on top of the first. Secular, clinical — nothing to believe in.
Ask What am I currently fighting that's true whether I like it or not? Write it as a plain statement of fact. Stating it plainly is the practice.
Choose Stoic · Dichotomy of control
Act from your values, not from reactivity. Spend energy only on what's yours: your judgments, choices, responses. Not other people, outcomes, or the past.
Ask One thing I'm spending energy on that isn't in my control — name it, set it down. One thing that is in my control — name it, and the action I'll take next week.

Why these three and not one journal. Free reflection under load drifts to the most activated thought and circles it. This sequence can't: every movement ends by handing the feeling somewhere — a named part, a stated fact, a chosen action. Movement, not marination.

Keep it short. If a movement runs to half a page, that's the rumination pull. Pull back to a few lines. The brevity is load-bearing, not laziness.

This one stays. The two-week writing intensive ends. This doesn't — it's a way of relating to your own mind, useful long after Molly is a settled chapter rather than an open one.
