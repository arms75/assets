Neck & Posture Reset

For forward head posture / chin-line correction
Why: forward head posture shortens the neck and compresses soft tissue under the jaw, creating a chin-line fold independent of body fat. Deep neck flexors (chin tucks) and upper-back strength pull the head back into alignment; extensors are already overactive, so they're deprioritised for now.
Daily essentials every day
Chin tucks
3 sets × 10–15, hold 5s. Draw chin straight back, no downward nod.
Wall angels
2 sets × 10. Head, upper back, hips flat to wall throughout.
Mon / Wed / Fri strength + stretch
Weighted neck flexion (bench)
3 sets, aim 12–15 reps. Face-up, head off end of bench, curl chin to chest. Currently capped ~12 — that's fine, let reps rise before adding load.
Doorway pec stretch
2 sets × 30s per side. Forearm on frame, step through gently.
Levator scap / upper trap stretch
2 sets × 30s per side. Tilt head toward armpit, light hand overpressure.
Thoracic extension over foam roller
1–2 sets × 8–10, slow. Roller across mid-back, extend gently over it.
Holding off for now Face-down weighted neck extensions. Feeling easy is expected — extensors are already overactive in forward head posture. Revisit once flexion reps climb past 15–20 comfortably and chin tucks feel automatic.
