Curiosity rotation — Block A

The History of Life on Earth, paced to run alongside draft season: one focused hour a week, video → reading → one applied step. Physics & Space (Block B) begins January 2027.

SLOT  Sunday 11:00–12:00
WEEKLY  1 h
RUNS TO  early December (matches the novel programme)

The three resources

  • Course spine — Dino 101: Dinosaur Paleobiology (Coursera, University of Alberta). Free, 12 lessons. Its set text is Brusatte, so reading feeds straight in.
  • Reading spine — The Rise and Fall of the Dinosaurs (Brusatte) → A Short History of Nearly Everything (Bryson) → The Rise and Reign of the Mammals (Brusatte).
  • Watch — PBS Eons (YouTube), specific ~10-min episodes named per week.

How the hour runs

  • 0–25 min — the week's Dino 101 lesson (or the named Eons episodes), take the lesson quiz where there is one.
  • 25–55 min — the assigned reading.
  • Last 5 min — one line in your rotation log: the single most surprising thing this week.
  • Behind? Watch only, skip the reading. Continuity beats completeness.

Essay threads — History of Life

  1. What does "deep time" do to your sense of human significance — humbling, freeing, or unsettling?
  2. Pick one extinction event. What's most surprising about what survived versus what didn't?
  3. If the continents hadn't drifted, how different might life look? Use one concrete example.
Lit-essay in spirit: a claim, evidence, and the connections you missed making — not a book report. Any prompt is abandonable the instant your own idea takes over.
